I am a first-year PhD student in Linguistics, Cultural, and Literary Studies at the University of Miami, with a focus on French and Francophone Literature. My research interests are in Postcolonial studies, Feminist studies, Caribbean studies, Digital Humanities, and Afrofuturism. I have my master's degree in French and Francophone literature from Purdue University. I am currently working on a digital project centered on Afrofuturist texts, The Afrofuturist Diaspora Chronos-Map. This project visualizes active futurism by mapping the past, present, and future through an analysis of an initial set of ten texts. This project is conceptualized as iterative and as a response to the criticism of Afrofuturism as mere escapism. Concurrently, I am preparing a paper titled "Curated realities: mirages of migration in Fatou Diome’s Le ventre de l’Atlantique," slated for presentation at the 51st Annual Conference of the African Literature Association.
